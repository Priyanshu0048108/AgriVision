Crop Details

1. Wheat (गेंहूं)

Scientific Name: Triticum aestivum

Growing Season: Rabi (Nov–March)

Ideal Conditions: Cool climate; loamy or clayey soil; temperature 10–25°C

Common Weeds: Wild oats (Avena fatua), Phalaris minor, Chenopodium album

Significance: Staple crop in Northern India; major contributor to national food security

Detection Tip: Most weeds look grass-like at early stages — needs fine segmentation

2. Rice (धान)

Scientific Name: Oryza sativa

Growing Season: Kharif (June–Nov), also Rabi in some regions

Ideal Conditions: High water requirement; warm temperatures; clayey soil

Common Weeds: Echinochloa crus-galli, Cyperus rotundus, Fimbristylis spp.

Significance: Lifeline for Eastern & Southern India; globally consumed

Detection Tip: Weed vs paddy plant distinction improves at 3-leaf stage

3. Maize (मक्का)

Scientific Name: Zea mays

Growing Season: Kharif & Rabi (depends on irrigation)

Ideal Conditions: Well-drained loamy soil; 18–27°C; full sunlight

Common Weeds: Amaranthus, Setaria, Sorghum halepense

Significance: Used in food, fodder, and biofuel

Detection Tip: Weed spacing is wider — good for image-based detection

4. Sugarcane (गन्ना)

Scientific Name: Saccharum officinarum

Growing Season: Feb–March (Spring), Oct–Nov (Autumn)

Ideal Conditions: Tropical climate, high water needs, rich loamy soil

Common Weeds: Nutgrass (Cyperus), Bermuda grass, Parthenium

Significance: Vital cash crop in Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra

Detection Tip: Weeds grow between wide rows — spacing-based planting points helpful

5. Mustard (सरसों)

Scientific Name: Brassica juncea

Growing Season: Rabi (Oct–Feb)

Ideal Conditions: Cool & dry climate; loam or sandy loam

Common Weeds: Chenopodium album, Convolvulus arvensis

Significance: Major oilseed crop in India

Detection Tip: Weed color often overlaps with mustard leaves in early stages

6. Cotton (कपास)

Scientific Name: Gossypium hirsutum

Growing Season: Kharif (May–June sowing)

Ideal Conditions: Dry climate, black soil, 21–30°C

Common Weeds: Amaranthus, Trianthema portulacastrum

Significance: Key textile crop; grown extensively in Central India

Detection Tip: Dense canopy later in season makes early detection crucial
